The Biography

Christopher George

A typical biography constitutes a sequence of details about one's upbringing, personal life, goals, hobbies, and other fluff which can easily obscure to the reader the actual identity of the subject of their reading.

It is important not to make the mistake of conflating identity with these things; Rather, what one does in their time is a reflection of their pattern of thought. And pattern of thought is identity. Writing clarifies identity because writing is formalized thought.
